Requirement gathering and Scope:
- Types of Vehicles: Car, Bike, Truck
- Parking spot types: compact, regular spots, and large.
- Parking Ticket: a ticket is issued with vehicle details and entry time when a vehicle enters. On exit, the system calculates the fee based on duration and vehicle size, then marks the spot as vacant.

Functional requirements:
- The parking lot has multiple parking spots
- The parking lot supports parking for motorcycles, cars, and trucks.
- Customers can park their vehicles in spots assigned based on vehicle size.
- Customers receive a parking ticket with vehicle details and entry time at the entry point and pay a fee based on duration, vehicle size, and time of day at the exit point.

Non-Functional requirements:
- The system should be scalable to accommodate more parking spots and vehicle types in the future.
- The system must reliably track spot assignments and ticket details to ensure accurate operations.

Entities:
- Vehicle: Represents a vehicle with attributes like license number, type (car, bike, truck), and size.
- ParkingSpot: Represents a parking spot with attributes like spot number, type (compact, regular, large), and availability status.
- Ticket - Represents a parking ticket with attributes like ticket number, vehicle details and entry time.

Behavioural Entities:
- ParkingLot: Represents the parking lot itself, acts as Facade class to manage parking spots and tickets,
    and provides methods for parking and exiting vehicles. All the operations related to parking and exiting vehicles will be handled by this class,
     ensuring a single point of interaction for clients. It acts as coordinator between functionalities, while the actual implementation is delegated.
- ParkingManager: Responsible for managing parking spots, assigning spots to vehicles, and tracking availability.
- The calculation of parking fees based on duration, vehicle size, and time of day will be handled by a separate class,
    FareCalculator, to adhere to the Single Responsibility Principle.
    This class will encapsulate the logic for fee calculation, allowing for easier maintenance and potential future enhancements without affecting other parts of the system.
    Since the logic/strategy to calculate fare will have different rules based on time of day, vehicle size, and duration,
    we can implement the Strategy design pattern to allow for flexible fee calculation strategies.
